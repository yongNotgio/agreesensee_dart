# **Phase 1: Farmer Registration and Farm Profiling**

### **Who is involved?**

**Farmer**

### **What happens?**

The first step is creating a farmer profile within the system.

The farmer enters important information such as:

* Full name  
* Contact details  
* Farm location  
* Total farm area  
* Barangay  
* Previous farming activities  
* Crops previously planted

### **Why is this important?**

This information allows the system to understand the farmer's situation and create a digital record that can be used for future planning and monitoring.

### **Result**

A complete digital farmer profile is created and stored in the system.

---

# **Phase 2: Crop Planning and Declaration**

### **Who is involved?**

**Farmer**

### **What happens?**

Before planting, the farmer informs the system about what crop they intend to plant.

The farmer provides:

* Crop type  
* Crop variety  
* Area to be planted  
* Planting date  
* Expected harvest date  
* Expected yield  
* Farm location

For example:

Juan plans to plant Ampalaya on a one-hectare farm in Barangay Igpaho starting on June 15\.

### **Why is this important?**

The system needs to know what crops farmers are planning to grow so it can forecast future production and identify possible oversupply situations.

### **Result**

The crop declaration is submitted and marked as **Pending Validation**.

---

# **Phase 3: Validation and Approval Process**

### **Why is validation necessary?**

Incorrect information can lead to inaccurate forecasts and poor agricultural planning.

To ensure data accuracy, every declaration passes through several levels of verification.

---

## **Step 1: Barangay Agricultural Worker (BAW) Validation**

The Barangay Agricultural Worker reviews:

* Farmer information  
* Farm location  
* Crop declaration  
* Uploaded farm photos

The BAW may:

* Approve the submission  
* Reject it  
* Request corrections

### **Purpose**

This serves as the first quality check to ensure the information is legitimate.

---

## **Step 2: Agricultural Technician Verification**

The Agricultural Technician performs a more technical review.

This may include:

* Checking whether the crop is suitable for the location  
* Assessing expected yields  
* Conducting farm visits if necessary

### **Purpose**

To ensure that production estimates are realistic and technically feasible.

---

## **Step 3: Municipal Agriculture Office (MAO) Approval**

After passing previous reviews, the declaration is forwarded to the MAO.

The MAO may:

* Approve  
* Archive  
* Monitor the declaration

### **Result**

Once approved, the crop declaration becomes an official part of the municipality's agricultural records and production forecasts.

---

# **Phase 4: Municipal Agricultural Data Collection**

### **What happens?**

After approval, AgriSense gathers information from all validated crop declarations and combines it with historical agricultural data.

This includes:

* Previous crop yields  
* Historical crop prices  
* Seasonal planting calendars  
* Production records from different barangays

### **Why is this important?**

The larger the dataset becomes, the more accurate the system's predictions and recommendations become.

### **Result**

A centralized agricultural database for the entire municipality is created.

---

# **Phase 5: Market Saturation Analysis**

### **What problem does this solve?**

One of the biggest challenges farmers face is oversupply.

When too many farmers plant the same crop, the market becomes flooded with produce, causing prices to drop significantly.

### **What does the system do?**

AgriSense compares:

**Expected Supply**  
 (How much produce farmers are expected to harvest)

versus

**Market Demand**  
 (How much produce buyers are expected to need)

### **Example**

If farmers are expected to produce:

* 900 tons of Ampalaya

But market demand is only:

* 500 tons

The system identifies a high risk of oversupply.

### **Why is this important?**

This warning allows farmers and agricultural planners to take action before losses occur.

### **Result**

The system generates a **Market Saturation Index** indicating whether a crop has a low, moderate, or high oversupply risk.

---

# **Phase 6: Crop Recommendation Engine**

### **What happens?**

Using all available information, the system evaluates:

* Land suitability  
* Current season  
* Market demand  
* Saturation risk  
* Historical profitability

### **Example**

A farmer plans to grow Ampalaya.

However, the system detects:

* High oversupply risk  
* Lower projected profits

The system may recommend:

* Eggplant  
* Okra  
* String Beans

instead.

### **Why is this important?**

Rather than simply telling farmers what is happening, the system provides practical alternatives that may lead to better income opportunities.

### **Result**

The farmer receives personalized crop recommendations before planting.

---

# **Phase 7: Financial Forecasting**

### **What happens?**

Before investing money into a crop, farmers can estimate whether it will be profitable.

The farmer enters:

* Seed costs  
* Fertilizer expenses  
* Labor costs  
* Irrigation costs  
* Transportation costs

The system then estimates:

* Expected revenue  
* Total expenses  
* Net income  
* Return on Investment (ROI)  
* Break-even point

### **Why is this important?**

Farmers can understand potential earnings before spending money on production.

### **Result**

A financial forecast report is generated.

---

# **Phase 8: Risk and Scenario Analysis**

### **What happens?**

Agriculture is uncertain because prices and yields can change.

To prepare farmers for different situations, AgriSense evaluates three possible outcomes:

### **Best Case Scenario**

* Higher crop prices  
* Higher yields

### **Expected Scenario**

* Average conditions

### **Worst Case Scenario**

* Lower prices  
* Lower yields

### **Why is this important?**

Farmers can better understand risks and prepare for unfavorable situations before planting.

### **Result**

A risk assessment report is generated.

---

# **Phase 9: Harvest Synchronization**

### **What problem does this solve?**

A common issue occurs when many farmers plant the same crop at the same time.

Because everyone harvests simultaneously:

* Supply suddenly increases  
* Market prices drop  
* Farmers earn less income

### **What does AgriSense do?**

The system analyzes:

* Planting schedules  
* Expected harvest dates  
* Crop types

It then predicts future harvest peaks.

### **Example**

If 200 farmers are expected to harvest Ampalaya during the same week, the system identifies a high-risk period.

It may recommend:

* Delaying planting dates  
* Choosing alternative crops  
* Practicing intercropping

### **Why is this important?**

This helps spread harvests over time and prevents market flooding.

### **Result**

A harvest synchronization schedule is generated.
